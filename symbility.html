Symbility Intersect

Lessons learned from working at a design agency

The Story

This past summer, I was a Product Design intern at Symbility Intersect.

Symbility Intersect is an agency specializing in mobile and IoT product strategy, design thinking, and engineering excellence for clients looking to build at the speed of startups. Their clients include Canada's largest companies including Northbridge Insurance, CIBC, TD Bank, LCBO, Indigo, Mastercard Canada, and more.

Throughout my 4-month internship, I had the opportunity to take ownership of multiple projects. The following is a outline of the cool things I did:

Claims Workflow Management Web Platform

  • Gathered requirements and wireframed potential solutions for numerous features such as registration, task management, and claim documentation
  • Prototyped every flow on InVision and presented them to the client for feedback
  • Incorported feedback received from the client and internal product reviews to produce high-fidelity mockups and assets for handoff

Accessible Style Guide

  • Independently created an AODA-compliant (Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act) styleguide for a big player in the human resources services and technology industry by conducting an audit on the existing platform and design system
  • Used the findings to redesign design componenents that still adhered to the client's brand guidelines and assembled a digital style guide outlining the specifications and best practices for usage

Illustration

  • Created illustrations and icons (for splash screens, landing pages, and surveys, to name a few) for multiple projects, which received excellent feedback from clients

Unfortunately, while I cannot share the work I did, contact me if you'd like to know more – I would love to chat in private!

In the meantime, the following are some lessons learned throughout my time at the agency.

Disclaimer: The following are my own thoughts and don't necessarily represent those of Symbility Intersect

Designing With Intent

With complex features, comes complex interfaces. Going through several design iterations of interfaces for multiple major key features on a workflow management platform has made me more mindful of how to lay out elements strategically so that the interface is intuitive and easy to interact with.

Asking Questions

At a design agency, it is pretty common to hop onto projects that have already been kickstarted or are in a later stage in the design process. I have learned to constantly challenge what has been presented to me and question everything.

Be Curious

It was exciting to work for an agency where many projects were going on simultaneously. Though I didn’t get the chance to touch on every single project, being naturally curious about what neighbouring designers worked on provided me with exposure on the different types of design problems that other industries face and allowed me to contribute in implicit ways.

Taking a Step Back

Projects under tight deadlines and immense pressure are not unusual for agencies. I occasionally found myself stuck on a design problem that did not seem to yield any elegant solutions. In situations like this, it is extremely helpful to take a step back and look at the problem holistically to realign user needs and end goals.

Owning Your Work

At Symbility, Product Review runs on a biweekly basis. Product reviews were a chance for designers to present their work, and provide and receive valuable feedback from other designers and product managers. Through numerous design-walkthroughs in client meetings and product reviews, I act as the owner of my work and take pride in my deliverables.

Practice Makes Closer-to-Perfect

One of my biggest goals this co-op term was to improve my visual design skills. During my internship, I was given the opportunity to perform plenty of visual design explorations that push my creative boundaries, while adhering to brand guidelines.
