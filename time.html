Time

Building habits and getting things done.

problem

the constant struggle with being productive.

As I found myself struggling to be productive during Reading Week, I wanted to dig deeper to identify the root causes of poor productivity and the best way to make the most out of the typical day. Using these findings, I decided to design an app that would identify such factors and provide users with insightful data that will enable them to make data-driven lifestyle changes to improve overall productivity.

research

what are the tricks to maintain focus?

Using myself as the primary research subject, I analyzed myself during times of poor productivity and narrowed down the root causes to 3 main factors:

Distractions:

  • Too much time is being spent on the computer and phone
  • Easily distracted and harder to focus after a long period of time

Fatigue:

  • Lack of sleep results in a significant decline in productivity
  • Fatigue deteriorates one's memory and chances of burning out increases

Mood:

  • Feeling demotivated and discouraged impacts one's drive drastically
  • Not the most productive when not at peak time (the time at which one feels the most energetic, creative, optimistic and enthusiastic)

In terms of being more productive with my time, I gathered the following tips:

  • Plan your day ahead of time to maximize the time you have in the day
  • Go about your day by using checklists or to-do lists. This allows you to prioritize your tasks and gives you a sense of accomplishment when you check a task off your list. It is recommended to limit yourself
    to 3 tasks per day to make your goals more attainable and stray from overwhelming you
  • Clear your mind and remove all negativity
  • Adopt the 90-30 minute technique or Pomodoro technique

workflow

how would the users be using this app?

To best accommodate for the target users, I flushed out the workflow to ensure that it would be as seamlessly integrated into the user's daily life as possible. This included figuring out when to prompt the user to track their sleep from the previous night, current mood, and top 3 tasks for the day.

Workflow of the app



It was decided that the user should be asked to track their sleep and prioritize their tasks as early as possible, so that the user can use the rest of the day to focus on their objectives for the day.

We often forget the different moods we feel throughout the day; therefore to ensure that these moods are recorded often, the user will be prompted to rate their current mood throughout the day at scheduled intervals.
