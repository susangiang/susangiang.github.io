Sprout Health Risk Assessment

Helping you identify your health risks and guiding you towards a healthier lifestyle

role

designer, internship, sept - dec 2016

Sprout is a leading Corporate Wellness Engagement platform that allows corporations to promote healthier and happier lifestyles for their employees, leading to higher productivity and overall satisfaction while on the job. Employees can use the Sprout platform to track activities, join challenges, and join communities consisting of employees that share similar interests.

For this project, I worked alongside the lead designer and two software developers. I was tasked with designing the overall flow and experience of the survey, for both web and mobile, to ensure that it is intuitive to use, quick to complete, and allows Sprout users to reap the benefits to its fullest.

context

being aware of your your risks, sooner

The Sprout Health Risk Assessment (HRA) is a survey in which users are asked a series of questions regarding their health and lifestyle habits. The intention of the survey is to determine risk factors that they may be especially prone to, and to provide recommendations on how they can reduce such risks, whether it be exercise suggestions or simply incorporating meditation into their daily routines. The HRA is a completely new feature; the major design challenges consisted of designing the flow of the actual assessment and determining how to incorporate the feature into the current state of the product to maintain consistency throughout the platform.

the goal

living a healthier tomorrow

The goal is to educate users of their health risks that they may be unaware of, and to provide them with recommendations on how they can mitigate such risks for a healthier and happier lifestyle.

the solution

Applying my knowledge of best practices for the design of web and mobile applications, I designed the screens for both experiences, presented them to the team, and after many iterations over a 1.5 month period, provided hi-fidelity mockups to the developers for implementation.

next steps

measuring impact

Onboarding is a pretty big deal. Like a first date, first impressions are lasting and sculpts the remainder of one’s experience. Some drastic changes were made to our onboarding flow, from the shifting of existing screens to the addition of the HRA, and we would be very interested in understanding how we have affected our users as a result, positively and negatively. Once the new onboarding flow makes a public release, some questions I would like our data to answer are:

Are users completing their HRA during their onboarding?

If not, which screens are the major drop-off points and what are possible reasons why they are choosing not to complete it?

Has the HRA feature encouraged the user to be more active in their lives?

Since the HRA score updates based on user step data collected, do users feel motivated to exercise more to improve their score? This can be determined by monitoring the user’s daily step data and activity tracking patterns.

Are users more aware of what features our platform offers, now that we have added value proposition screens, and are they taking the initiative to visit the respective pages on the platform?
